# Method: Distill Subject (step 4)

_The repeatable process behind `subject-observed.md`. This is the IP — the output is just one run of it. First drafted from the Artifacts dogfood, 2026-06-28._

## Goal
Produce the agent's own **evidence-backed** read of *what a newsletter is about* — sharp and falsifiable, not generic. Powers Milestone 1.

## Inputs
- The corpus (raw issues preferred; summaries OK for subject, NOT for voice).
- The stated identity (about page) and the user's intake — held aside, used only in step 5 reconcile. **Do not read them first** (anchoring, Q7).

## Process

1. **Read the whole corpus before concluding anything.** No skimming to the first pattern.
2. **Extract along fixed axes** — but fill them with *specifics*, never adjectives:
   - **Through-line** — the implicit argument across issues (one sentence).
   - **Topic clusters** — ranked by weight, each with ≥2 issue citations.
   - **Lenses** — recurring *ways of seeing* (distinct from topics). The hardest and highest-value to spot.
   - **Motifs / fingerprints** — self-coined terms, repeated questions, signature concerns.
   - **Audience** — inferred from assumed knowledge + register.
   - **Boundaries** — what it conspicuously *avoids* (look for explicit disavowals).
   - **Formats** — the structural types in rotation.
3. **Cite or cut.** Every claim names the issues that prove it (D11). A claim you can't cite is a hypothesis — drop it or mark it.
4. **Separate topic from lens.** "Covers AI regulation" is a topic; "reads interfaces as political texts" is a lens. The lens is what makes the read feel *true to this writer* and not generic.
5. **Flag corpus fidelity.** If issues look condensed/summarized, note it and quarantine them from voice work.

## Anti-slop tests (run before shipping the read)
- **Substitution test:** would this sentence be true of *any* newsletter in the genre? If yes, it's slop — make it specific or cut.
- **Citation test:** every claim traceable to named issues.
- **Surprise test:** does the read contain ≥1 thing the writer might *not* have already said about themselves (a latent lens/motif)? If not, you've only mirrored, not distilled.
- **Disagreement surface:** does it expose ≥1 gap vs. the stated identity, to feed step 5?

## Output
`subject-observed.md` — structured read + a reconcile preview (gaps vs. stated/said). Hand to step 5 (reconcile), then step 6 (Brief).
