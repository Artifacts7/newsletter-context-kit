# Method: Newsletter Brief (step 6) — the source of truth

_The Milestone 1 deliverable. The core document all newsletter work revolves around.
Drafted 2026-06-28._

## Purpose (the goal)
A concise, living document that captures **what the newsletter is about, how it's been
written so far, and its objectives & vision.** It is:
- the **source of truth** every later step (voice, ideation, drafting) is checked against;
- the thing that **keeps the writer honest** as they produce — does this issue serve the brief?
- the document you **deliberately change on a pivot** (not drift into silently).

## Inputs
`subject-observed.md` (shown) · voice-character (shown) · `seed-context.md` (said) ·
`identity-stated.md` (stated) · `reconcile.md` (the resolved gaps + aspiration).

## Mode & length (D20)
Write it as a **confident identity document**, not an analyst's report. Declarative ("Artifacts
is…", "treated as…"), not hedged ("by weight," "the corpus shows"). **No one-screen limit** —
a source of truth earns 1.5–2 pages. **Quality bar: match or beat a strong hand-written
CLAUDE.md** (explicit topics, *how each is treated*, named patterns). The richness from
`subject-observed.md` belongs IN the Brief — don't compress it out. Issue names go inline as
light provenance, not as the main text.

## Structure
```
# <Newsletter> — Brief   (+ the one-line tagline)
_Source of truth. Living doc. Last confirmed: <date>._

## The thesis            — developed paragraph + the signature structural move
## What it's about — and how each topic is treated
   - each topic: what it is · HOW the newsletter treats it (the angle) · a representative issue
   - call out side veins vs. pillars explicitly
## The patterns          — the recurring analytical moves (built-for-vs-now, etc.), with examples
## Signature vocabulary  — the self-coined terms / motifs that ARE the identity
## How it's been written so far — formats · structure · voice character (descriptive; ops rules → M2)
## Objectives & vision   — where it's heading (from reconcile) · what success looks like
## Boundaries & audience — what it avoids · who it's for
## Open tensions         — unresolved / pivots, parked and visible
```

## The gate (what makes it the source of truth)
The Brief is **not** done until the writer confirms it: "yes, this is my newsletter,
and yes, that's where I want it to go." Present it, invite correction, revise. Partial-
but-confirmed beats complete-but-imposed.

## Rules
- Distinguish **is** from **becoming** — descriptive vs. aspirational, never blurred.
- Every "what it's about" claim traceable to the corpus (D11).
- Mark anything still awaiting the writer's reconcile answers as **provisional**.
- Living: stamp the last-confirmed date; on a pivot, edit the Brief first, then the work.
