# Method: Intake / Describe (step 1)

_The conversational elicitation that captures what the corpus and about-page **can't**.
Output: `seed-context.md`. Drafted 2026-06-28._

## Goal
Get the writer's own words — the non-derivable signal — before any machine reading.
Low-effort: a short Q&A, not a blank page (the cold-start fix).

## Rule: ask only what can't be derived
The corpus gives topics/voice; the about-page gives the stated positioning. **Don't ask
for those.** Ask for what's only in the writer's head. If a question could be answered by
reading their archive, cut it.

## The question set (5–7, keep it short)
1. **Punchline.** Your newsletter in one sentence.
2. **A few lines.** What it is, who it's for, the thesis — as you'd tell a friend.
3. **Reader.** Who do you picture reading it? (one real person is better than a demographic)
4. **Never.** Topics or angles you'd *never* touch.
5. **Proud vs. disown.** One issue you're proud of, one you'd disown today. _(quality bar + drift signal)_
6. **Becoming.** Where do you want it to go — what do you want it to *become*? _(seeds aspiration for reconcile/step 5)_
7. **Other homes.** Where else does your material live (Notion, Drive, notes)? _(source pointers for later phases)_

## Output template → `seed-context.md`
```
# Seed context (the writer's own words)
**Punchline:** …
**What it is / who it's for / thesis:** …
**Reader:** …
**Never:** …
**Proud of:** …   **Would disown:** …

**Other knowledge homes:** …
_Captured: <date>. The writer's self-description — held as one of three views
(said) for the reconcile step (5), alongside stated (about) and shown (corpus)._
```

## Anti-friction notes
- Accept terse answers; don't interrogate. Partial is fine — the Brief is iterable.
- Never block on this: if the writer skips a question, note it and move on.
- This is question-asking, not form-filling — conversational, one or two at a time.
