# Method: Reconcile (step 5) — the engine

_Turns three static views into directional context. Drafted 2026-06-28._

## Inputs (the three views, from Phase A)
- **said** — `seed-context.md` (the writer's own words)
- **stated** — `identity-stated.md` (the about page)
- **shown** — `subject-observed.md` + voice-character (what the corpus actually does)

## Process
1. **Three-way diff.** Lay the views side by side. Find where they disagree. Four gap types:
   | Gap | Meaning |
   |---|---|
   | said, not shown | claims a focus the corpus barely supports |
   | shown, not said | corpus is full of something the writer never names |
   | stated vs. shown | about-page positioning ≠ recent practice |
   | said vs. stated | self-description ≠ public positioning |
2. **Prioritize.** Surface the **top 3–5** gaps only (overwhelm kills the gate). Pick the ones that most change the Brief.
3. **Ask, don't assert (Q7 — anchoring).** For each gap, ask one *open* question. Not "You focus on X, right?" but "Your corpus leans hard on X but you didn't mention it — core, or a habit you'd break?"
4. **Classify by the answer:**
   - *"where I want to go"* → **aspiration** → feeds Objectives & Vision
   - *"I drifted / not me anymore"* → **drift** → down-weight, flag for prune
   - *"didn't realize"* → **latent strength** → surface in the Brief
   - *"noise"* → **exclude**

## Why it's the engine
The corpus is **descriptive** (what the newsletter *was*). This dialogue is the **only**
place **aspiration** (what it *wants to be*) enters. Without it the Brief is a mirror, not a compass.

## Output
`reconcile.md` — the gaps, the questions, and (once answered) the classifications. The
aspiration/latent items flow into the Brief's "objectives & vision" and "what it's about."
