# Editorial review — the substance & intention pass

The missing middle of the writing loop: between drafting and language polish, an editor's read of
whether the piece *works* and *serves what the newsletter is for*. Grounded in the **Brief**, so it's
about the overall picture, not the prose.

## Reads
`newsletter-context/newsletter-brief.md` (thesis · lenses · objectives · boundaries · formats) · the corpus/manifest
(substance-level overlap). **Not** the voice profile — that's `polish`'s job.

## Procedure
1. **Intention / fit.** Does the draft serve the thesis and at least one **objective**? Has it
   *drifted* — or strayed into a topic the newsletter avoids (boundaries)? Say so honestly; this is
   where the Brief keeps the writer honest.
2. **The interface moment.** Is there a concrete screen/feature/decision leading the piece? Is it
   sharp — and is it clear **who decided** and what power is in play? (the core test.)
3. **The argument.** Is there a real *"So, what?"* — a pivot from reportage to idea — or is it just
   news? Does one of the signature **lenses** (built-for-vs-now, commission/omission…) do real work?
4. **Altitude & structure.** Right depth; does open → pivot → close hold; does the close circle back;
   is the governing metaphor **real and load-bearing**, not decorative or manufactured?
5. **Substance overlap.** Has this argument been made in a past issue? (cite slugs) — deepen or
   differentiate, don't repeat.
6. **Coherence & gaps.** What's missing, what's unclear, where it sags.

## Output
An editor's read: **what works first**, then prioritized notes on what's weak / missing / off-brief.
The writer acts on them.

## Boundary (D21)
A structural editor, not a ghostwriter or copy-editor. It does **not** rewrite. Pairs with `polish`:
**substance & intention here, voice & language there.**

---
_**Language:** if the newsletter isn't in English, converse with the writer — and write outputs — in the newsletter's language._
